How It Works

From clutter to clean in three taps.

STEP 1

Bring in the recipe

Paste a URL, drop in a screenshot, pick a photo from your library, or scan a cookbook page with the camera. Whatever's easiest.

STEP 2

Tap Import

Simmer Down extracts the recipe — on-device, no servers — and saves a clean version in your library. No login, no signup.

STEP 3

Cook

Open the recipe to a clean ingredients-and-steps view. Send the ingredients to your shopping list with one tap, or share the recipe with a friend.

Features

Everything that ships in v1.

  • Recipe URL import: Pulls ingredients and steps from JSON-LD recipe markup (used by most modern recipe sites) and from WPRM-style recipe blocks. No screen scraping of unrelated content.
  • Smart screenshot import: Paste or pick a screenshot of a recipe — Apple Intelligence and Vision OCR read it on-device and extract a structured recipe. Great for Facebook posts you can't otherwise grab.
  • Choose photo from library: Import a recipe from any photo on your device — cookbook page, recipe card, handwritten note. Same on-device extraction pipeline.
  • Scan with camera: Point the camera at a recipe and pull it in. Works on cookbooks, magazine clippings, recipe cards, and the kitchen-table whiteboard.
  • Paste text from clipboard: Copy a recipe from Notes, an email, a Reddit comment — anything text-shaped. Apple Intelligence pulls out the structured recipe.
  • Clean recipe view: Title, ingredients, steps. That's it. Serif typography that's easy to read at arm's length on the counter.
  • Auto-import on save: Forget to tap Import? If you save a recipe with just a URL, Simmer Down imports automatically.
  • Drafts that don't disappear: Start a recipe, switch apps, come back hours later — your in-progress edits are still there. Even after iOS terminates the app.
  • Share to anywhere: Native iOS share sheet with the full formatted recipe ready to paste into Messages, Mail, Notes, AirDrop, or any other app you've installed.
  • Favorites pinned to top: Star recipes you cook often. They float to the top of the list and can be filtered with one tap.
  • Shopping list: Standalone or generated from a recipe's ingredients. Check off items as you shop, reorder by dragging, clear the "done" pile in one tap.
  • Family sharing: Create a family with a 6-character code. Anyone you share the code with can join and sync recipes + shopping list.
  • Member roster: See every device in your family in Settings. Rename your device, remove a member's access with one tap.
  • Rotate the invite code: If a code has been shared too widely, generate a new one. Old code stops working immediately; existing members keep their access.
  • Cooking Mode: Keeps the screen awake on recipe pages so your phone doesn't lock with floured hands. Toggle in Settings.
  • iPad split view: On iPad and Mac, the recipe list sits in a sidebar with the detail in the main pane.
  • Mac app: Same app, full-window experience. Family syncs across phone, tablet, and desktop.
  • On-device AI, no servers: All the smart-extraction work runs entirely on your device using Apple's Foundation Models and Vision frameworks. Nothing about your recipes is sent to LH Vantage or any third party for AI processing.
