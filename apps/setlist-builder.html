How It Works

Three taps to a real playlist.

STEP 1

Search the artist

Type the band name. Setlist Builder looks them up on setlist.fm and shows you their recent tour activity.

STEP 2

Pick your window

Choose a date range — the last 30 days for the current tour, or the full career for a sampler.

STEP 3

Send to your library

Pick Apple Music or YouTube Music. The playlist appears in your library, ready to play.

Features

What's in the app.

  • setlist.fm integration — Searches the crowdsourced live-music database for any artist's recent setlists. The artist name is the only thing sent to setlist.fm.
  • Frequency-weighted ranking — Songs played most often across recent shows are ranked first, so the playlist matches what you'll actually hear live.
  • Custom date range — Tune the analysis window from a single tour leg to an artist's entire career.
  • Apple Music playlists — Creates a real playlist in your Apple Music library via MusicKit. Requires an active subscription.
  • YouTube Music playlists — Creates a playlist on YouTube Music via Google sign-in and the YouTube Data API. Free tier of YouTube Music works fine.
  • Song-match verification — Before adding each song, the app checks for a matching track in the destination service so the playlist isn't littered with wrong-version covers.
  • MusicBrainz + iTunes fallbacks — Cross-references song metadata when setlist.fm titles are ambiguous.
  • Cover art — Pulls album art from MusicBrainz with an iTunes fallback so the playlist looks right.
  • Recent searches — Saves your last few artist lookups locally for quick re-runs. No remote storage.
  • Local-only data — Everything stays on your device. No accounts, no servers, no analytics.
🔒

Local-only by design. Setlist Builder doesn't have servers. Recent searches stay on your device. Auth tokens for Apple Music or YouTube Music are stored only in your iOS Keychain.
